Unit 14

In this unit, I learned about the importance of installing and maintaining computer hardware. I found out how keeping hardware in good condition can help a technology system work better and last longer. I also learned that people who do these jobs—like computer technicians or helpdesk engineers—need to know how to find problems and fix them.

As part of the unit, I planned and carried out some hardware tasks. I opened up a computer to find and repair internal faults, like checking if the processor or memory was working properly. I also upgraded parts of the system by adding things like extra RAM or a network card.

Then, I installed a new external device, such as a printer or scanner, and made sure it worked with the system. After everything was set up, I tested the system to see if it was working well. I checked if it was faster or more reliable than before.

Finally, I reviewed the work I had done, looked at how well it met the goals, and got feedback from a client to see what could be improved.
